Alexander L. Posey

A Creek Humorist in Early Oklahoma

Alex. Posey was an American poet, humorist, journalist, and politician in the Creek Nation. He founded the Eufaula Indian Journal in 1901, the first Native American daily newspaper.

    Timeline of Alex. Posey's life:

  • 1873 - Born in Eufaula, Oklahoma
  • 1885 - At the age of 12, learned to speak and write English
  • 1890 - Attended Indian University at Bacone
  • 1892 - Published his first poem in "The Instructor", a faculty newsletter at the University.
  • 1895 - Graduated from BIU and entered politics. He was elected to a seat in the Creek National Council "House of Warriors" representing the Wind Clan. In this same year, he met Miss Minnie Harris of Fayetteville, Arkansas. She was a school teacher he hired to act as Matron of the Creek orphan home.
  • 1896 - Took a position as Superintendant of the Creek Orphan Asylum at Okmulgee. Alex and Minnie married later that year.
  • 1897 - Alex resigned as Superintendant of the orphanage and was appointed as Superintendant of Public Instruction of the Creek Nation. He and Minnie returned to the area where he spent his childhood and moved back to his farm near Stidham, Oklahoma.
  • 1901 - Alex took editorial charge of "The Indian Journal" in Eufaula and began production of the poems he had previously written after he and Minnie had married.
  • 1902 - He began writing and publishing the humorously satirical "The Fus Fixico Letters"
  • 1905 - He was appointed as Secretary for the Sequoyah Constitutional Convention. He was credited for writing most of this constitution.
  • 1906 - Took a job with the United Indian Agency as a Clerk for the Creek Enrollment Field Party to search for the Lost Creeks
  • 1908 - Untimely death at the age of 34 by accidental drowning in the North Canadian River. He is buried at Greenhill Cemetery in Muskogee, Oklahoma.
